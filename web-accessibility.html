Web Accessibility

Images

You know that little alt attribute in HTML image tags? USE it! If your photo fails to load or if someone is visually impaired and is using a screen reader to view your website, the computer will say what the picture is supposed to be.

<img src="puppies.jpg" alt="Picture of puppies">

Script loading

Imagine trying to visit a website, and having to wait minutes for the page to load bit by bit. For users with slow internet connections, this can be a common problem. To avoid this, try improving your website loading speed by loading your scripts after loading your content.

Other ways to improve performance is to reduce image file sizes and minify your code.

Mobile friendly

In 2016 mobile usage surpased desktop usage and continues to grow. To make sure you target the most users, be sure to design your website to work well from a mobile platform. To do this, it is recommended to take a mobile first approach and style for desktop after finishing styling for mobile.

Readability

For those who are visually impaired or have dyslexia, it can be hard to navigate some websites if they are poorly designed. To make your website easy to read, consider using a font color that contrasts with your background, use an off white background, and use sans-serif versus serif fonts.
